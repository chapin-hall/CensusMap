Introduction

Welcome to the Census Mapping Tool.

This tool allows you to select your own geography.

Estimated statistics are computed from the Census Bureau based on your selected geography.

Directions

First draw your geography using the icon on the bottom right corner of the map.

If your geography is discontinuous, you may draw multiple shapes.

Once you have created your geography of interest, click the "Run" button to compute your aggregated estimates.

To start over, either click the "Remove" button to clear your selected geographies or refresh the page.

View

Click the Results Tab to see your results

About

Methodology

Data is drawn from the 2010 Census

Your selected geography is matched to corresponding Census Tracts

An estimate is computed for your geography using the corresponding Census Tracts

For example, if your geography contains 10 Census Tracts, these tracts would be summed together to get the corresponding estimated statistic

If your geography is only a partial match to a Census Tract, the percent overlap of the two geographies will be used as the percent to add to the estimate statistic

For example, a census tract that 10% within the selected geography will contribute 10% of its value to the overall estimated statistic

Results are displayed in the Results Section
